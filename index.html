Beyond the Classroom

I believe that true education happens as much on the sports field, the stage, and in the corridors of the boarding house as it does at the chalkboard.

I see my role as creating a safe, structured environment where young people learn the value of accountability, community, and self-respect.

High standards require high support.

Whether I am guiding a student through a difficult equation or a difficult personal decision, I am there to provide the firm boundaries they need to feel secure, and the relational warmth they need to thrive.

My Approach

Academic Rigour & Clarity

Mathematics is often a source of anxiety for students. I turn it into a source of confidence. My classroom is a space where "I can't" is replaced by "I haven't solved it yet." By breaking down complex concepts into logical steps, I help students not only master the curriculum but develop the critical thinking skills they will use for the rest of their lives.

Community & Belonging

A boarding house is more than a place to sleep; it is a home. I strive to create an environment that balances the warmth of family with the structure of a disciplined community. My goal is for every boy to feel known, safe, and valued - understanding that his actions impact his brothers. We celebrate diversity, demand mutual respect, and build lifelong bonds.

Guidance & Growth

Adolescence is a pivotal transition. As a mentor, I walk alongside students as they navigate the pressure of expectations and the search for identity. Whether on the sports field or in a quiet one-on-one conversation, I challenge them to look beyond the immediate and envision the men they want to become - grounded in integrity and driven by purpose.
