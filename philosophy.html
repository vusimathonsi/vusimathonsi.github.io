My Philosophy

I believe that academic performance begins with knowing the student. Trust breeds effort - when a student feels seen, they work hard for you, and eventually, they learn to work hard for themselves.

Read more

My 3 Pillars

Restorative Discipline

I never raise my voice. Instead, I raise questions. My approach to accountability is to ask the student to examine the thinking that led to their actions, and then connect those actions to the community's consequences. I monitor that sanctions are fulfilled, but more importantly, we evaluate the "why" afterwards. We handle with care, always.

Metacognitive Maths

In my classroom, we focus less on the "how" (procedure) and more on the "when" and "why" (strategy). I use open-ended questioning to push students to think deeply rather than just memorise. By building trust first, I create a safe space where students can struggle with difficult equations without fear, turning anxiety into competence.

Service-driven Innovation

I don't believe in building tools for the sake of technology; I build them to fill gaps in care. When our school needed a better way to track attendance, I coded a solution. When detention management needed streamlining, I built a system. If a tool doesn't exist to help me serve my students or colleagues better, I am keen to build it myself.

The 5-Year Goal

Ultimately, I hope that a student under my guidance learns to lead with empathy, holds themselves to higher standards, and builds the habits that set them up for a lifetime of success.
